Never assume the AI knows the exact brand name or specific geographical landmark unless it is world-famous. Adding just three words of context can make the difference between a generic tag and a high-selling keyword.

### Batch Processing Like a Pro ###

To get the most out of your metadata generator, you must adopt a batch-processing mindset. Never upload, tag, and submit one photo at a time. Instead, group your images by shoot, location, or visual theme before running them through the software.

Processing similar images together ensures consistency across your portfolio. It also allows you to review and apply bulk changes much faster. If the AI generates a fantastic keyword for one image, you can easily verify it was applied to the rest of the batch.

Exporting a single, massive CSV file for 500 images is infinitely faster than dealing with 500 individual text files. Platforms like Adobe Stock make it incredibly easy to upload your jpegs alongside a properly formatted CSV spreadsheet.

Automation does not mean you should completely abandon quality control. Always take five minutes to quickly scan the generated keywords before submitting them to an agency. You are looking to remove any accidental inaccuracies that could flag your account for spam.

Sometimes, an AI might mistake a reflection in a window for a second person, adding irrelevant tags. Deleting these minor errors ensures your portfolio maintains a high trust score with the stock platform's review team.

Think of the AI as your incredibly fast assistant. It does ninety-nine percent of the heavy lifting, but you are still the art director who gives the final approval. This quick review process guarantees top-tier quality every single time.
